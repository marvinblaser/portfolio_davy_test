Master 1 | Chemistry

Multi-step Synthesis of Coordination Networks

Objective

Total synthesis and purification of a complex functionalized alloxazine-based tecton for the self-assembly of porous coordination networks (MOFs). The challenge was integrating redox-active motifs capable of modulating electron transfers within organized molecular architectures.

Methodology

  • Synthesis Engineering: Execution of a four-step synthetic sequence: chemoselective reduction using NaBH4 (0°C), bifunctional catalysis-assisted double condensation, double N-alkylation, and Palladium-catalyzed Suzuki-Miyaura coupling.
  • Operational Mastery: Manipulations under inert atmosphere (Argon) using a Schlenk line, reflux heating (100°C), and purification via silica gel column chromatography.
  • Characterization: Systematic structural elucidation of intermediates and the final ligand through 1H NMR spectroscopy (MestReNova), Infrared (ATR), and UV-Visible.
"Multi-step synthesis is a lesson in humility. I learned that an insignificant stoichiometric discrepancy can redefine the trajectory of an entire crystallization."

L3 | Polymers

Alginate Hydrogels & Controlled Release

Objective

Design and optimize biopolymer vectors (alginate beads) capable of encapsulating and releasing an active substance in a programmed manner. The challenge was to understand how the gel structure and the addition of additives influence diffusion kinetics.

Methodology

  • Polymer Engineering: Synthesis of hydrogel beads via ionic gelation (cross-linking of sodium alginate in a CaCl2 solution).
  • Hybrid Formulation: Integration of activated carbon within the polymer matrix to increase adsorption capacity and delay release.
  • Kinetic Study: Real-time monitoring of release using UV-Visible spectrophotometry at different temperatures to model diffusion fluxes.
"I realized that the architect can dictate time. By adjusting porosity, I could slow down or speed up a process invisible to the naked eye."

L3 | Materials

Inorganic Ceramics & Spinels

Objective

Synthesize complex oxides with a spinel structure (NiAl2O4) using soft chemistry methods and develop traditional ceramics. The challenge was to correlate heat treatment parameters (up to 1280°C) with the crystalline microstructure.

Methodology

  • Sol-Gel Synthesis (Pechini): Powder preparation via the citrate method to ensure atomic homogeneity and high phase purity.
  • Thermal Engineering: Management of calcination and sintering cycles in high-temperature furnaces to induce crystallization.
  • Characterization: Rigorous phase analysis by X-Ray Diffraction (XRD) and Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA).
"Temperature is an atomic 'sculpting' tool. Controlling heat allows dictating the arrangement of ions within the crystal lattice."

M1 | Green Chem

CO2 Valorization into Fuels

Objective

Analyze technological solutions for capturing carbon dioxide (CO2) and transforming it into strategic raw materials (Methanol, Polymers).

Methodology

  • Capture Technologies: Comparative study between chemical absorption (amines) and physical adsorption (Zeolites 13X).

L3 | Chemoinformatics

Molecular Modeling: PfATP4

Objective

Explore the structure and function of the PfATP4 protein (malaria target) using numerical simulation to understand resistance mechanisms against inhibitors like Cipargamine.

Methodology

  • Bioinformatics: 3D structure reconstruction using AlphaFold2/Swiss-Model and sequence alignment (BLAST).
  • Molecular Docking: Simulation of ligand binding using AutoDock Vina to predict stable poses and binding energies.
  • Visualization: Manipulation and rendering of complex biological structures in a Linux environment using PyMOL and VMD.
"The computer is a construction tool as powerful as the lab bench. Before physically building matter, we can now model and test it virtually."
