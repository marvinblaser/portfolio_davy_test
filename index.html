Master 1 | Chemistry

Multi-step Synthesis of Coordination Networks

Objective

To perform the total synthesis and purification of a complex functionalized alloxazine-based tecton for the self-assembly of porous coordination networks (MOFs). The challenge was to integrate redox-active motifs capable of modulating electron transfers within organized molecular architectures.

Methodology

  • Synthesis Engineering: Execution of a four-step synthetic sequence: chemoselective reduction using NaBH4 (0°C), bifunctional catalysis-assisted double condensation, double N-alkylation, and a Palladium-catalyzed Suzuki-Miyaura cross-coupling.
  • Operational Mastery: Manipulations under inert atmosphere (Argon) using a Schlenk line, reflux heating (100°C), and purification via silica gel column chromatography with dry loading.
  • Advanced Characterization: Systematic structural elucidation of intermediates and the final ligand through 1H NMR spectroscopy (processed via MestReNova), Infrared (ATR), and UV-Visible.
  • Self-Assembly: Engagement of the final tecton in solvothermal crystal growth with zinc salts to generate 3D coordination polymers.
"This project forged my patience and surgical precision. Multi-step synthesis is a lesson in humility: I learned that an insignificant stoichiometric discrepancy can redefine the trajectory of an entire crystallization."

L3 | Polymers

Alginate Hydrogels & Controlled Release

Objective

To design and optimize biopolymer vectors (alginate beads) capable of encapsulating and releasing an active substance in a programmed manner. The challenge was to understand how the gel structure and the addition of additives influence diffusion kinetics.

Methodology

  • Polymer Engineering: Synthesis of hydrogel beads via ionic gelation (cross-linking of sodium alginate in a CaCl2 solution).
  • Hybrid Material Formulation: Integration of activated carbon within the polymer matrix to increase adsorption capacity and delay the release of methylene blue.
  • Kinetic Study: Real-time monitoring of release using UV-Visible spectrophotometry at different temperatures to model diffusion fluxes.
  • Data Analysis: Correlation between physical parameters (temperature, carbon concentration) and the efficiency of the release system.
"I learned that chemistry is not limited to transforming molecules but also involves organizing matter in space. By adjusting the 'porosity' of my beads, I realized that the architect can dictate time."

L3 | Materials

Inorganic Ceramics & Spinels

Objective

To synthesize complex oxides with a spinel structure (NiAl2O4) using soft chemistry methods and to develop traditional ceramics. The challenge was to correlate heat treatment parameters (up to 1280°C) with the crystalline microstructure and the final properties of the material.

Methodology

  • Sol-Gel Synthesis (Pechini): Powder preparation via the citrate method to ensure atomic homogeneity and high phase purity.
  • Thermal Engineering: Management of calcination and sintering cycles in high-temperature furnaces to induce crystallization and material densification.
  • Crystallographic Characterization: Rigorous phase analysis by X-Ray Diffraction (XRD) and calculation of lattice parameters to verify Vegard's Law.
  • Thermal Analysis (TGA): Monitoring of physicochemical transformations and mass losses during temperature rise.
  • Industrial Ceramics: Shaping of clay plates and study of their structural evolution.
"I learned that temperature is an atomic 'sculpting' tool: by controlling heat, I could dictate the arrangement of ions within the crystal lattice."

IT | Engineering

Custom Hardware & Home NAS

Objective

To take on the technical challenge of designing and building a custom-made computer from scratch. The goal was to gain a precise understanding of the internal mechanics to achieve the most optimized price-to-performance ratio for academic research and high-end leisure.

Methodology

M1 | Green Chem

CO2 Valorization into Fuels

Objective

To analyze technological solutions for capturing carbon dioxide (CO2) and transforming it into usable resources. The challenge was to understand how to convert industrial waste into a strategic raw material for methanol and polymer production.

Methodology

  • Capture Technologies: Comparative study between chemical absorption (amines) and physical adsorption (Zeolites 13X), evaluating energy constraints.
  • Fuel Conversion: Analysis of catalytic hydrogenation processes for the production of methanol, a clean energy carrier.
  • Polymer Synthesis: Study of CO2 copolymerization with epoxides (ROCOP process) to create polycarbonates for long-term carbon storage.
"Chemistry is not just about creating new molecules; it is the primary tool for closing the carbon cycle. It strengthened my ability to evaluate the real-world viability of a technology."

L3 | Chemoinformatics

Molecular Modeling: PfATP4

Objective

To explore the structure and function of the PfATP4 protein—a vital sodium pump for the malaria parasite—using numerical simulation tools. The challenge was to characterize molecular interactions with inhibitors (e.g., Cipargamine) to understand resistance mechanisms.

Methodology

  • Bioinformatics: 3D protein structure reconstruction using AlphaFold2 and Swiss-Model, plus sequence alignment (BLAST) to identify functional domains.
  • Molecular Docking: Simulation of ligand binding using AutoDock Vina, smina, and rxdock to predict stable poses and binding energies.
  • Visualization: Manipulation and rendering of complex biological structures in a Linux environment using PyMOL and VMD.
  • Dynamics Prep: Parameterization of complex systems (ligand-protein-membrane) via CHARMM-GUI.
"I discovered that the computer is a construction tool as powerful as the lab bench. We can now model and test matter virtually to achieve ultimate precision."
